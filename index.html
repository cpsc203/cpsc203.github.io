CPSC 203

Programming, Problem Solving, and Algorithms

A few of you may be wondering what this course is about. Well, the technical answer to that is it's about the analysis of increasingly complex algorithmic problems, using a modern programming language and a variety of approaches. Problem decomposition and abstraction guide explorations of topics from applied algorithms ... But what does that really mean? Well loook no further ... really ... just look to your right and you can scroll through some explanations of what this means.

What language will you be learning?

The language that will be used in this course is Python just like in CPSC 103

How will we begin?

Well, by knitting ... okay ... so you're probably wondering KNITTING?!? But knitting is a cool analogy to function decomposition and for loops (AKA iterations).

Will we be learning about Panda?

Yes! Okay, not the animal type but the one that makes data analysis easy by looking at scraped data from the Billboard Top 100 (Ah yeah! Some cool music analysis).

So where will we be applying algorithms?

Good question! One example will be with Voronoi Diagrams. That cool image there. We will talk about how it's used with tree growth and art! Art? You'll see :)

Will we learn how to generate a random song?

Yes! Through applying Markov Chains!

How will we improve on our problem solving?

By solving a puzzle! (Like Sudoku). We will build an algorithm to do it for us!

Will we plan a trip?

Yes, we'll attempt to find an optimal route for planning a road trip!

Will we be entering the wizarding world?

Yes! We'll figure out the popularity of Harry Potter through NLTK and figure out his social network (like FACEBOOK!)
